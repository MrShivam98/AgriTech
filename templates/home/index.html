Stubble burning is a practice of removing paddy crop residues from the field for sowing next cropAbout 25 % of nitrogen (N) and phosphorus (P), 50 % of sulfur (S) and 75 % of potassium (K) uptake by cereal crops are retained in crop residues. Further, it is estimated that burning of paddy straw results in nutrient losses viz., 59,000 t of nitrogen, 20,000 t of phosphorus, 34,000 t of potassium and 3.85 million tonnes of organic carbon. So, it is clear now that crop residue is not a waste but rather a useful natural resource, Therefore it is necessary to get rid of stubble.
